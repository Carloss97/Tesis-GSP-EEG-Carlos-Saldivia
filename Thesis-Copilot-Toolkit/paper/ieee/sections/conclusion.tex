\section{Conclusion}
We provide a reproducible, evidence-anchored comparative study for reconstructing missing EEG channels using graph-based and temporal methods. Core numerical claims are traceable to repository artifacts (notably \showresfile{results/it100_final_comparative_publication_pack_run_metadata.json} and \showresfile{paper/ieee/tables/b3_rep01_final_table.tex}). Future work includes completing strict cross-stack numerical closure against MATLAB/GSPBox; until then we follow INS-13 and label cross-stack checks as \texttt{proxy\_or\_partial} with \texttt{strict\_close=False}.

Overall, the evidence supports TV/time methods as a robust choice in many EEG scenarios, particularly when temporal continuity is expected; nevertheless, the multidomain extension demonstrates that final method selection should be conditioned on dataset and scenario (see the comprehensive iteration report \showresfile{Thesis-Copilot-Toolkit/ITERATIONS_COMPREHENSIVE_REPORT.md} and the per-iteration artifacts in \showresfile{paper/ieee/figures}). The repository and the included traceability tables enable readers and reviewers to reproduce the reported findings and to rerun targeted iterations if tighter domain-specific confirmation is required.
