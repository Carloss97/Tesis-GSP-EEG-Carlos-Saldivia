\section{Conclusion}

This paper presented a reproducible benchmark comparing geometric baselines, graph-based interpolation, and temporal regularization methods for EEG reconstruction under realistic missing-channel conditions.

The main empirical finding is a performance crossover that becomes remarkably clear after hyperparameter optimization using Optuna. The temporal regularization method TRSS achieves unprecedented reconstruction quality, with an average MAE of \ResTVMAE\ and SNR of \ResTVSNR~dB, vastly outperforming geometric baselines such as Spherical Spline (MAE $\approx$ \ResBaselineMAE, SNR $\approx$ \ResBaselineSNR~dB). This difference is statistically significant across datasets ($p < 0.05$).

At the method level, TRSS and Tikhonov regularization demonstrate robust superiority over purely spatial graph methods and independent component analysis (ICA). TRSS not only minimizes spatial error but excels in preserving signal dynamics (DTW $\approx$ \ResTVDTW), proving that incorporating temporal smoothness into the graph Laplacian framework provides a definitive advantage in EEG reconstruction.

The extensive validation across PhysioNet, BCI Competition IV 2a, and MNE Sample datasets confirms the generalizability of these findings. Future work will explore the computational efficiency of these optimized algorithms for real-time Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) applications and investigate their integration with deep learning pipelines.
