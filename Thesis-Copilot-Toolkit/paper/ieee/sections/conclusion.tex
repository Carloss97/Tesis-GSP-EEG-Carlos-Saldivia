\section{Conclusion}

This manuscript reported a reproducible EEG channel-reconstruction benchmark that compares baseline, graph-based, and TV/time methods under shared protocols and full artefact traceability. The central result is a metric-dependent crossover: Baseline achieves the lowest mean MAE/RMSE, while TV/Time achieves the lowest mean DTW. Family-level differences are statistically significant under STAT-02.

The evidence therefore supports a conditional recommendation instead of a universal ranking. For amplitude-oriented objectives, baseline and graph-aware instant methods remain competitive. For temporal-dynamics preservation, TV/Time methods are preferable.

A strict limitation remains mandatory: BGSRP is validated only in a controlled Python proxy and must be reported as \texttt{proxy\_or\_partial} with \texttt{strict\_close=False}. No strict 1:1 MATLAB/GSPBox equivalence claim is currently supported.

Future work should prioritize strict cross-stack BGSRP closure, expand validation on additional real EEG cohorts, and tighten scenario-adaptive model selection rules for deployment-oriented pipelines.
