\section{Related Work}

Prior work on graph interpolation established foundational approaches for missing-value recovery in graph-structured data. Narang and Ortega introduced graph-based interpolation formulations for structured signals, while Puy \emph{et al.} studied sampling and recovery conditions for bandlimited graph signals.

More recent methods include RKHS-based recovery schemes such as BGSRP, and temporal formulations that exploit smoothness over both graph and time dimensions. In EEG settings, these ideas are relevant because channels are spatially arranged and physiological activity is temporally correlated.

Our work differs by providing a single benchmark framework that compares these families under shared data, masking, and evaluation protocols.
