\section{Discussion}
The experimental evidence shows method performance depends on dataset, graph construction, and missing-data scenario. The canonical pack (\showresfile{results/it100_final_comparative_publication_pack_run_metadata.json}) highlights \texttt{\ItHundredBestMethod} as best by the selected MAE metric and reports a substantial median improvement (\texttt{gain\_pct} = \ItHundredGainPct\%, $p=\ItHundredPval$). However, editorial REP-01 aggregates (\texttt{b3\_rep01}) indicate close competition between TV/time and GSP smoothing in terms of win counts, illustrating that no single family dominates across all scenarios.

INS-13 compliance and limitations: when cross-stack comparisons with MATLAB/GSPBox were executed they are documented as controlled proxy checks and must be read under the project's editorial policy (canonical status: \texttt{proxy\_or\_partial}, \texttt{strict\_close=False}); see \showresfile{results/ins13_strict_status.md} and the comparison summary \showresfile{results/ins13_strict_matlab_compare_summary.csv}. Importantly, the multidomain extension (it105--it120) produced several NO-GO aggregated outcomes — these are discussed in the full report and used to qualify conclusions rather than overturn them (see \showresfile{Thesis-Copilot-Toolkit/ITERATIONS_COMPREHENSIVE_REPORT.md}).

Practical implications: for EEG use-cases where temporal continuity is expected (e.g., continuous recordings, cognitive tasks with smooth dynamics), TV/time methods consistently reduce MAE and produce more faithful temporal reconstructions (evidence: multiple packed figures such as \showresfile{paper/ieee/figures/it100_final_comparative_publication_pack_fig07.pdf} and the per-iteration signal-reconstruction PDFs). Conversely, in some high-variability multidomain scenarios the advantage is reduced and method selection must be domain-aware.

Future work and editorial guidance: the repository includes recommended flags and lightweight re-runs (it121–it130 runner scripts) to verify domain-specific claims; these are suggested when authors need tighter confirmation for a particular dataset or deployment scenario.
