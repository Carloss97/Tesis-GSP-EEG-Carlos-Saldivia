\section{Results}

The B3/B4 closure used the consolidated B1+B2 ranking to build a final publication
table (REP-01), plus significance tests (STAT-02) over the full-scale B2 raw data.
The family-level summary confirms that baseline methods obtained the lowest average
MAE, while TV/time methods achieved the lowest average DTW.

\midrule
Baseline & 0.071270 & 0.521934 & 0.162150 & spherical\_spline \\
TV/Time & 0.072925 & 0.491819 & 0.167430 & tv \\
GSP & 0.073695 & 0.498893 & 0.168976 & gsmooth \\

\label{tab:b3_rep01_summary}
\end{table}

STAT-02 tests reject $H_0$ for the key family contrast instant vs TV/time on both
MAE and DTW (Wilcoxon paired), indicating that family-level differences are not
explained by noise alone under the current protocol. At method level, the contrast
between TRSS and BGSRP is significant, while TRSS vs GSP is not significant in MAE,
which supports a scenario-aware interpretation instead of a single universal winner.
