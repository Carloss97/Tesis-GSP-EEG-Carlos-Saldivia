\section{Experimental Setup}

Experiments follow a fixed pipeline: data loading, graph construction, missing-channel simulation, hyperparameter optimization, reconstruction, and quantitative evaluation.

For the final validation phase, we executed a comprehensive benchmark across multiple domains to ensure generalizability. 

\subsection{Datasets}
We evaluate the methods on three distinct, publicly available datasets:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{PhysioNet EEG Motor Movement/Imagery Dataset}: Real 64-channel EEG data representing actual clinical-grade recordings.
\item \emph{BCI Competition IV 2a}: A standard 22-channel motor imagery benchmark dataset.
\item \emph{MNE Sample Dataset}: A 60-channel dataset capturing auditory and visual evoked potentials.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Masking Protocol}
Masking simulates both systematic and sporadic sensor failures. We use two spatial modes: \emph{random} (electrodes fail independently) and \emph{nearby} (spatially clustered failures). For each mode, we vary the degradation severity using \emph{ratios} (10\%, 20\%, 30\%, 40\% of total channels) and discrete \emph{counts} (1, 2, or 3 specific channels missing).
