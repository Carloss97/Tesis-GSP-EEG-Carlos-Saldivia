\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a graph signal processing and interpolation framework for EEG channel reconstruction under missing-channel scenarios. We compare graph construction methods and interpolation families, including geometric baselines, graph-based estimators, and temporal regularization methods. The experimental protocol evaluates reconstruction quality with MAE, RMSE, DTW, and SNR across synthetic and benchmark-like configurations. Results show that graph-informed approaches improve reconstruction fidelity over purely geometric baselines under realistic masking conditions.
\end{abstract}
