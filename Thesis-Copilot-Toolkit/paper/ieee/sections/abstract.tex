This paper studies EEG channel reconstruction under missing-channel conditions using a reproducible graph signal processing benchmark. EEG electrodes are modeled as graph vertices, and reconstruction quality is evaluated with MAE, RMSE, DTW, and SNR under shared scenario-driven masking protocols. The B3/B4 closure confirms a metric-dependent crossover: baseline methods lead on MAE/RMSE, whereas TV/time methods lead on DTW, with statistically significant family-level differences. Extended evidence across it02-it130 is incorporated under strict GO/NO-GO policy, avoiding the use of NO-GO runs as confirmatory support. The BGSRP status is reported with mandatory INS-13 wording: proxy-or-partial validation in Python with strict\_close=False, and strict 1:1 MATLAB/GSPBox equivalence still open.
