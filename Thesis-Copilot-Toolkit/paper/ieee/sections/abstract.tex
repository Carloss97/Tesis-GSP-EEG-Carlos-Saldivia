\begin{abstract}
Missing EEG channels remain a critical bottleneck in both clinical neurophysiology and Brain-Computer Interface pipelines because interpolation errors can distort morphology, latency, and spectral content of downstream biomarkers. This paper studies EEG channel reconstruction under missing-channel conditions using a multi-dataset Graph Signal Processing (GSP) framework. While conventional geometric baselines typically focus on spatial smoothing, recent graph-based estimators incorporate graph topology, and advanced methods add temporal regularization (TV). To ensure a rigorous and fair evaluation, we use a unified optimization-and-validation pipeline over an exhaustive experimental registry with scenario-driven masking protocols across three datasets (PhysioNet, BCI Competition IV 2a, and MNE Sample). Our consolidated quantitative analysis (MAE, RMSE, DTW, SNR, LSD, and coherence) demonstrates a significant paradigm shift: temporal regularization methods, particularly TRSS and Tikhonov, substantially outperform exhaustively optimized geometric baselines. Furthermore, TRSS excels at preserving critical physiological dynamics, successfully recovering Event-Related Potentials (ERP) and Power Spectral Density (PSD) even under severe contiguous channel loss, thus establishing a robust and reproducible reference for EEG interpolation.
\end{abstract}
