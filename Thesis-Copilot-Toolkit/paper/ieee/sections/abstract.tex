\begin{abstract}
Missing EEG channels represent a significant challenge in clinical neurophysiology and Brain-Computer Interface pipelines because interpolation errors can distort morphology, latency, and spectral content of downstream biomarkers. This paper studies EEG channel reconstruction under missing-channel conditions using a multi-dataset Graph Signal Processing (GSP) framework. While conventional geometric baselines typically focus on spatial smoothing, recent graph-based estimators incorporate graph topology, and advanced methods add temporal regularization (TV). To ensure a rigorous and fair evaluation, we use a unified optimization-and-validation pipeline over an exhaustive experimental registry with scenario-driven masking protocols across three datasets (PhysioNet, BCI Competition IV 2a, and MNE Sample). Our consolidated quantitative analysis (MAE, RMSE, DTW, SNR, LSD, and coherence) shows that temporal regularization methods, particularly TRSS and Tikhonov, achieve competitive performance compared to exhaustively optimized geometric baselines. Furthermore, TRSS demonstrates strong capability in preserving physiological dynamics, successfully recovering Event-Related Potentials (ERP) and Power Spectral Density (PSD) even under severe contiguous channel loss, thus providing a useful reference for EEG interpolation.
\end{abstract}
