\begin{abstract}
We present a reproducible, evidence-first comparative study of graph-based and temporal reconstruction methods for missing EEG channels. Experiments were executed on canonical synthetic and real/proxy datasets (including synthetic\_16ch, synthetic\_32ch, mne\_sample\_proxy, bci\_competition\_proxy and physionet\_eegmmidb) and across a wide set of graph constructions and interpolation families. The canonical publication pack (tag: \showtag{it100\_final\_comparative\_publication\_pack}) consolidates per-iteration artifacts and reports a median improvement of \ItHundredGainPct\% (field \texttt{qa.gain\_pct} in the pack metadata) for the leading method, with statistical significance ($p=\ItHundredPval$); the best method in the pack is \ItHundredBestMethod{} (best MAE = \ItHundredBestMae) — full run metadata: \showresfile{results/it100_final_comparative_publication_pack_run_metadata.json}.

All quantitative claims are traceable to repository artifacts (CSV, per-iteration PDFs and scripts). Cross-stack checks against MATLAB/GSPBox are reported under INS-13 as \texttt{proxy\_or\_partial} with \texttt{strict\_close=False} (see \showresfile{results/ins13_strict_status.md}). A compact traceability table is included in the paper and the full run-level table is available as supplementary material in the repository.
\end{abstract}
