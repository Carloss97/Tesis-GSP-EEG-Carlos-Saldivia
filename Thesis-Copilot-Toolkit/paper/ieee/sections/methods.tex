\section{Methods}

We represent EEG channels as graph vertices and model inter-channel relationships with weighted adjacency matrices. At this stage, we focus on a practical and reproducible benchmark implementation, not yet on claiming a single theoretically optimal method.

\subsection{Graph Construction}
We evaluate several graph constructors implemented in our pipeline, including neighborhood and adaptive alternatives. In the B2 stage, we prioritize methods that stayed numerically stable during full-scale execution.

\subsection{Interpolation Families}
We compare three families:
\begin{itemize}
\item Instant methods (e.g., linear, idw, gsp, gsmooth, tikhonov, bgsrp).
\item TV/time methods (e.g., trss, tv, graph\_time\_tikhonov).
\item Reference geometric/spline baselines.
\end{itemize}
The goal is to compare behavior under the same masking protocol and metrics, instead of tuning each method independently for best-case numbers.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Performance is assessed with MAE, RMSE, DTW, and SNR. We report mean and standard deviation, and for consolidated B2 analysis we also track approximate 95\% confidence intervals from repeated runs.
