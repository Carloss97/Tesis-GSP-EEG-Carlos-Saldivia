\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

This thesis studies EEG missing-channel reconstruction from a graph signal processing perspective, comparing baseline methods, graph-based instant methods, and TV/time methods under a common and reproducible protocol.

The B3/B4 closure consolidated quantitative evidence through traceable REP-01 and STAT-02 artefacts. Results show a metric-dependent crossover: Baseline achieves lower mean MAE and RMSE, whereas TV/Time achieves lower mean DTW. Therefore, method selection should be objective-aware and scenario-aware rather than based on a universal ranking.

The extended it02--it130 campaign is integrated under explicit GO/NO-GO policy: only GO runs are treated as confirmatory evidence, while NO-GO runs are used to characterize limits and risk. In addition, INS-13 constraints are enforced: BGSRP is reported as proxy\_or\_partial with strict\_close=False, and strict 1:1 MATLAB/GSPBox equivalence is not claimed.

The main contribution is a reproducible and auditable methodological base for publication, together with a concrete closure agenda to strengthen external validation and cross-stack equivalence.
